# Khandera Art Space

Khandera Art Space is a community-driven platform dedicated to fostering creative expression, critical engagement, and social solidarity. Based in Girirajpur, Khandera, Dadri, Uttar Pradesh, India, the space was officially started on April 14, 2023, coinciding with Ambedkar Jayanti.

The space has been collectively shaped by the villagers, with the initiative emerging particularly through the efforts of local stakeholders Neetu and Deep Chand. The initial collaborative process of establishing the space began in March 2023 and included Neetu, Deep Chand, research scholars Labani Jangi and Vedant Patel, MFA students Khusboo Biswal, Arzu Tarafder, Shiv Shankar, Sayak Mohanta, and Tathagata Paul, along with other villagers from Girirajpur and contributions from Anupam Roy.

The work at Khandera Art Space is grounded in the five principles of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar: *“Be educated, be agitated, be organised, be confident, never give up.”* Adhering to these principles, the space seeks to create a site of cultural resistance and a discursive space through art and dialogue.

Khandera Art Space emerged as a direct response to the socio-political and environmental crisis present in the Dadri region of Uttar Pradesh. Engagement in this area has been ongoing for over two years, shaping both the vision and the practices of the space.

In this context, the goals of Khandera Art Space are multiple. The initiative aims to forge an alternative space for political expression in a region where such avenues remain scarce. By harnessing artistic practices, the space works to articulate voices of resistance while amplifying the environmental concerns of the new generation.
